▶ Episode Summary: Trading Around a Core Position [28:00]

Generate consistent returns by scaling out on 5% rallies and scaling back in on weakness, keeping a long-term core holding.

  • Core position method: buy 25 shares four times to reach 100 shares of Nvidia (or any stock) gradually, avoiding lump-sum risk.
  • Once you own 100 shares, sell 25 shares on every 5% gain to lock in profits; never sell the final 25 (your permanent core).
  • When weakness strikes and nothing changes with the thesis, buy back 25 shares on every 5% decline; can accumulate beyond 100 if price drops far enough.
  • Small gains add up over time; trading around a core position is boring but prudent—not reckless gambling.
  • Works especially well in volatile markets where great companies get tossed around by hedge fund leverage and mechanical selling.
