▶ Interview: Regeneron (REGN) [24:07]

Regeneron CEO Schiff: Dupixent (8 indications, leader in 7) is pipeline-in-a-product; eylea back strong with longer dosing intervals; AI has limits, internal R&D beats acquisitions.

  • Dupixent: approved for 8 indications, leading in 7; used by 1M+ people globally; dermatologists, allergists, pulmonologists, gastroenterologists, ENTs all see benefit—safe, effective, doctors believe in it.
  • Eylea (retinal): most durable anti-VEGF therapy; $2B/year run rate, nearly half of Regeneron franchise; doctors love longer injection intervals (vs. 52x/year previously).
  • Libtayo (non-melanoma skin cancer): 68% reduction in cancer recurrence vs. Keytruda's 'big fat goose egg'—shows Regeneron's technology differentiation.
  • AI limits: AI can optimize data associations from millions of sequences linked to medical records, but won't invent drugs or think about technology application; won't create antibodies that Regeneron already knows how to make.
  • Internal R&D model: 14 drugs from Regeneron's own labs approved—record. Acquisitions rarely work; return on investment is poor; bankers inflate prices in auctions (Regeneron will pass at $1 if deal fetches $8).
  • Proluent (cholesterol combo): lowers LDL by 50-60% (vs. obesity drugs' ~10%); paired with obesity drug creates game-changer—half of obese people also have high LDL; potential to be one of most-prescribed drugs in history.
