▶ Interview: RBN Energy CEO Rusty Brazil – Venezuela Oil Reality Check [29:11]

Venezuelan crude reserves overstated; current production only 900K bbl/day with broken refinery infrastructure—U.S. oil market far less vulnerable than headlines suggest.

  • Venezuela reserves on paper huge but reserves may be exaggerated; current production 900K bbl/day (down from 3-4M/day years ago)—only ~1% of world production.
  • Heavy crude requires upgraders or diluent ('paint thinner') to refine; not the same as light sweet crude; much harder and more expensive to process.
  • Venezuelan refinery capacity down to ~20% operational from historical levels; most crude now shipped to U.S. or China for processing, not refined in-country.
  • Even if Trump's target of 30-50M barrels delivered in 12-18 months, only equals 300-400K bbl/day flow—manageable, not transformational for U.S. crude markets.
  • Permit crude is superior (closer via pipeline, fewer processing needs) than Venezuelan; will remain more valuable for U.S. refiners in medium term.
  • Refiners invested billions expecting Venezuelan heavy crude volume 10-15 years ago, but production collapsed; shale freed them from that dependency; won't pay off until decade ahead.
