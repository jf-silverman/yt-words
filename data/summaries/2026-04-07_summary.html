▶ In-Depth: McCormick (MKC) [27:34]

McCormick's massive Unilever food deal (Hellmann's, Knorr, Colman's) transforms scale and shelf power; market hates it but fundamentals compelling.

  • Stock down 42% over past 5 years, worst month in 46 years (March -29%); packaged food group deeply unpopular amid weight-loss drug and macro worries.
  • Announced reverse Morris Trust with Unilever Foods: McCormick gets premium brands (Hellmann's mayo, Knorr soups, Colman's mustard, Maille Dijon); existing MKC holders end up with 35% of combined company.
  • Deal creates $20B+ revenue platform (vs $7B standalone) with 600M annual cost synergies by year 3; improves international exposure (Europe, Middle East, Africa, Brazil, China vs historically domestic focus).

  • Risks: huge balance sheet impact, unusual structure, deal not closing until mid-2027, regulatory uncertainty. But after stock collapse, worries priced in; Cramer likes the deal as transformational move for packaged food.
