▶ Interview: Sterling Infrastructure (SLG) [13:00]

SLG transformed from low-margin highway work to high-margin data center infrastructure; 1,800% 5-year gain justified.

  • CEO Joe Catillo pivoted company from 90% low-bid heavy highway (low margin, high risk) to data center site development and electrical work (high margin, high value)
  • Data center projects are massive 'campuses' with multi-year buildouts; Sterling handles underground infrastructure, site flattening, exterior electrical (duct banks, substations), and interior electrical—$300-400M site packages plus $500M per building electrical
  • Each data center building requires 300-500 electricians; shortage of skilled trades addressed through premium wages (60-hour weeks), own university, trade school recruitment, and largest equipment fleet
  • M&A critical: bought CEC (Dallas, electrical/semiconductor expertise) to marry outside electrical with site development, shaving months off project timelines and guaranteeing on-time delivery
  • Philosophy shift: margin and cash flow growth over revenue growth; drives company toward high-growth, high-value specialty markets where they deliver unique value (project certainty, technology/AI/drones on jobsites)
  • Stock up 1,800% over 5 years, 250% over 12 months, 40% YTD despite recent quarter being strong; Cramer very bullish and looking to add to charitable trust
