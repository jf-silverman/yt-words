▶ Interview: CrowdStrike (CRWD) [11:02]

CrowdStrike CEO George Kurtz: Anthropic's Mythos AI requires cybersecurity partners to safely deploy; AI adoption depends on security.

  • Anthropic developed Mythos, a frontier AI model adept at finding zero-day vulnerabilities; launched Project Chimera with tech firms & cybersecurity experts (incl. CrowdStrike, Palo Alto Networks) to patch flaws before release.
  • Zero-day vulnerabilities: software flaws unknown even to developers; thousands go undetected for years/decades. AI can find and chain them together across code bases, exposing massive exploits if weaponized.
  • Kurtz: CrowdStrike is not replaceable by AI; the company is a net-data creator (security telemetry from millions of endpoints globally). This proprietary data moat of 15+ years enables AI training and defense.
  • Key insight: AI adoption bottleneck is security. C-suite customers measuring AI adoption rates and grading teams on speed; securing AI so they can go faster is the #1 priority at Kurtz's customer advisory board.
  • Threat model: AI will accelerate attack discovery (5 days to working exploit → 5 minutes). Need mitigating controls like CrowdStrike during patching cycles.
  • Two types of cybersecurity companies: (1) those that just visualize/slice data (disruptible by AI); (2) net-data creators like CrowdStrike with massive moat (sticky, hard to replace).
  • Kurtz: Defending against AI-powered adversaries requires partnerships; CrowdStrike gains full-power access to Mythos without guardrails, tilting LLM advantage toward defenders.
