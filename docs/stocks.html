Reading the chart

Each point on the blue line is the closing price on an episode date — the day Cramer made that call. Points are shaped and colored by sentiment: ▲ bullish (Strong Buy, Buy, Mild Buy, Buy on Pullback), ● neutral (Wait / Hold), and ▼ bearish (Caution/Concern, Sell/Avoid). Hovering a point shows the date, price, sentiment, and Cramer's note.

The ■ light purple bars are the full daily closing price history, giving context between episode dates. Use the From / 30d / 90d / To now controls to zoom into a specific window.

% Days Right vs. % Right (Analytics tab)

% Days Right (shown in the card header) measures how consistently the stock moved in the direction Cramer predicted — day by day. For each call, every trading day from that call until the next call (or until the latest available daily price for the most recent call) is checked: was the closing price above the call-day price (for buy calls) or below it (for sell calls)? The percentage is the share of those days where the answer is yes, across all directional calls for this ticker. Neutral calls (Wait / Hold) are excluded — only bullish and bearish calls count.

% Right in the Analytics tab is a different calculation: it asks whether the stock was higher (for buy calls) or lower (for sell calls) at a fixed point in time — exactly 30 or 90 calendar days after the call. It's a single snapshot per call, not a daily tally. A call can score 100% in Analytics if the stock closes higher on day 30, even if it spent most of the intervening period below the call price.
