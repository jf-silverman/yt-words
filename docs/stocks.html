Reading the chart

Each point on the blue line is the closing price on an episode date — the day Cramer made that call. Points are shaped and colored by sentiment: ▲ bullish (Strong Buy, Buy, Mild Buy, Buy on Pullback), ● neutral (Wait / Hold), and ▼ bearish (Caution/Concern, Sell/Avoid). Hovering a point shows the date, price, sentiment, and Cramer's note.

The ■ light purple bars are the full daily closing price history, giving context between episode dates. Use the From / 30d / 90d / To now controls to zoom into a specific window.

% Days Right After Call vs. % Right (Analytics tab)

% Days Right After Call (shown below each ticker's chart) measures how consistently the stock moved in the direction Cramer predicted — day by day. For each call, every trading day from that call until the next call (or until the latest available daily price for the most recent call) is checked: was the closing price above the call-day price (for buy calls) or below it (for sell calls)? The percentage is the share of those days where the answer is yes. It's broken out by Buy calls and Sell calls separately, plus a Combined figure blending both — splitting them out keeps a ticker with lopsided coverage (mostly buy calls, say) from having its sell-call accuracy averaged away. Neutral calls (Wait / Hold) are excluded from all three — only bullish and bearish calls count. The gray (Nc) next to Buy calls / Sell calls is the number of individual calls behind that percentage, separate from (Nd), the number of trading days tallied — every call counts toward the stat no matter how few days passed before Cramer's next call on that stock, so a fast reversal doesn't quietly disappear from the record.

Outcome After Hold / Wait Call covers what the stat above leaves out: a hold/wait call makes no directional claim, so there's nothing to score as "right" or "wrong" without an arbitrary judgment call about whether avoiding a loss is worth the same as missing a gain. Instead it reports both outcome rates side by side, using the same day-by-day window as above: the share of days the price Fell after the call (a loss avoided) versus Rose (a gain missed) — each with its own day count, plus the number of hold/wait calls behind them in gray next to the panel title — you weigh the two by your own risk tolerance rather than trusting a single score. Shows N/A rather than 0% when there's nothing to compute: either no hold/wait calls at all, or a call with no trading days after it yet.

Any of the figures above tagged Caution — small sample are backed by fewer than 30 tallied days — the same threshold used elsewhere on this site for "small sample" warnings. Treat those percentages as directional, not reliable.

% Right in the Analytics tab is a different calculation: it asks whether the stock was higher (for buy calls) or lower (for sell calls) at a fixed point in time — exactly 30 or 90 calendar days after the call. It's a single snapshot per call, not a daily tally. A call can score 100% in Analytics if the stock closes higher on day 30, even if it spent most of the intervening period below the call price.
